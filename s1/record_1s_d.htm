GUIDE FOR USING THE CATALOGUE

 

                The present parts catalogue covers diesel counter-balanced engine driven lift trucks of the range "S1" with rated load capacity 1250 kg (1630), 1500 kg (1632), 1750 kg (1636); 2000 kg (1638) engine DEUTZ, transmission 6861.2 S3, single drive wheels, lifting equipments as follows:

- two-stage mast with low free lift (simplex) and maximal lifting height h=30 and 40 dm.

- two stage mast with extended free lift (duplex) and maximal lifting height h= 30 dm;

                - three stage with full free lift (triplex) and maximal lifting height h=42, 45, 50, 54 and 60dm.

Type designation of the fork lift truck is:

                               1630.h.a, where:

                h - max lifting height in dm;

                a - type of the truck.

                The catalogue includes the component parts of the trucks. If it is necessary to the basic catalogue will be enclosed parts catalogue for the engine, complementary equipments, etc.

                The  catalogue  is  based on  up-to - date   designer   documentation (2009)

                For user convenience all components are grouped by their function. Group (and subgroup) designation and references are given in the Group Index section.

                The illustrations and the lists to them cover components (dismountable parts, units, assemblies and systems) of the respective groups.

                All components are shown in assembly succession, sometimes in two or more lines connected by center line. This arrangement indicates also the order of truck component stripping and assembly. In each illustration truck system components are referred to by item numbers.

                The lower RH corner of the illustration contains the system reference number, year of compilation (or updating) of the catalogue, and in the lower LH corner is given the system (group) function in the truck. The designation is given in the centre.

                All group components are listed in one or more pages with item number catalogue reference, denomination, quantity and indication. There is a note next the indication for the parts which are not common for all models. It shows to which model or lift height the part belongs. When some positions  are missing in the text it means that these parts don't belong to the concrete type truck.

 

 

Different position with identical indications means that the parts in the concrete type fork lift truck are identical.

                The following abreviations and symbols are used in the text, lists and illustrations:

M 01.00 -  a truck group whose components are shown in illustration M 01.00 and listed in list M 01.00

 

                HOW TO ORDER PARTS

                Every order must contain the following information:

                1. The type, the serial number and the year of production taken from the truck nameplate and the service book.

                2. The full name of the catalogue and the year of publication.

                3. The group (subgroup) of the respective illustration and item number of the part.

                4. Denomination and indication of the part. When the denomination is given in the second or in the third language, it must includes the type designation given in the first language.

                When  ordering complete units, assemblies or products must indicate the respective generalized catalogue reference.

                5. Part quantity required. When the order is placed by cable give quantity in words as well.

                6. Delivery address.

                When the above requirements are not met, all expences for wrongly delivered parts will be borne by the buyer.
